## Recommendation: **defer formula edits — formal trigger 3 substance check fails**

Rationale (additive to the n=8 / n=11 rationales, which still hold):

1. **Trigger 3 fired mechanically (n>15) but did not fire substantively
   (no boundary erosion).** The L4 brief above 7.500 (flood-fusion at 7.695)
   passes the three-pillar test cleanly; its high composite is driven by
   exceptional implementation (7.75) and hardware (9.25), not by a
   defensibility shortcut. The L4 cluster's other five briefs sit ≤ 7.009,
   well below the L1 main cluster — the lane boundary is more visible at
   n=16 than it was at n=9, not less.
2. **Per-axis ranges have stretched on every axis** as the cluster diversified
   into L2 and added new L4 verticals. Financial range +56% (1.500 → 2.333),
   implementation +17% (1.500 → 1.750), hardware +20% (2.500 → 3.000). The
   compression concern that motivated the deferral discussion at n=4/n=9
   is materially weaker here.
3. **σ ≈ 0.40 at n=16 is statistically indistinguishable from σ 0.384 at n=9.**
   The distribution shape has not changed; the cluster has stretched in
   range while keeping the same dispersion profile around its center.
4. **L2 cluster tightness (range 0.335 across n=5) is a feature, not a bug.**
   Every L2 brief is structurally capped by the conditional-moat ceiling
   that the cerebrum codifies (off-platform archive + demonstrated
   precedent). L2 briefs DON'T differentiate strongly on composite — they
   differentiate on the prose moat-thesis (which incident, which buyer, which
   detail-layer carve-out). Operator decisions on L2 briefs will be driven
   by the Lane-2 prose much more than by composite ranking, and that's the
   correct operator workflow per the spec §6.
5. **Comparability cost remains binding.** Graduated `07.221-somd-cameras`
   anchors the formula. Any weight tweak ripples through 15 candidate
   composites; the rename surface is non-trivial (every candidate brief
   filename embeds the score).
6. **No operator-decision evidence to drive an edit.** Zero approved briefs;
   trigger 1 cannot fire without operator decisions. The earliest plausible
   data point is the first approval cycle (operator-only transition), and
   the formula should be re-evaluated against that real signal rather than
   against synthetic projections.

stretching by +1 on five of six sub-criteria) suggests that the
existing implicit anchoring is converging on the right band as scorers
