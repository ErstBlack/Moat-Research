## Discovery story

`fdot_fl511_cameras` was identified during the 2026-05-06 T1 discovery synthesis
pass (iteration `20260506T094308Z-dd2ef2`) as the highest-priority geographic gap
in the existing DOT-camera corpus. All existing Lane-1 camera briefs cover either
the Pacific Coast (Caltrans CA, WSDOT WA, ODOT OR), the Southwest/Texas interior
(TxDOT), or the mid-Atlantic (somd MD/VA, NJDOT NJ). Florida — the 3rd most
populous US state, home to three major seaports, and the terminal point of the
I-95 SE freight corridor — has no coverage in the current corpus.

The marine-freight angle distinguishes FL511 from every existing camera entry.
Port of Miami, Port Everglades, and Port Tampa Bay are three major containerized-
cargo and petroleum seaports whose approach roads (I-95/SR-836/I-395/I-195 for
Miami; SR-595/I-595/US-1 for Port Everglades; I-75/I-4/US-41/SR-60 for Tampa)
generate specific cargo-routing intelligence unavailable from any other DOT-camera
corpus. The Port of Miami is the largest US cruise port and a significant cargo
hub; Port Everglades handles petroleum terminals for Chevron, ExxonMobil, and Shell.
None of the Pacific Coast or mid-Atlantic camera briefs have a marine-terminal
approach-road focus comparable to what Florida's three ports represent.

The iCone/511-portal platform shared with NJ511 makes FL511 architecturally the
most straightforward new camera entry in the corpus after the initial URL-template
discovery step — no developer-token registration (vs. WSDOT), no Google AppEngine
backend concern (vs. TxDOT), and a real robots.txt with clear camera-path
allowance (vs. ODOT's absent robots.txt which required a conservatism note).

## §5 Lane-1 verification

Per the cerebrum CONSTRAINTS §5 verification methodology:

1. **Upstream archive paths.** Checked fl511.com and fdot.gov for any
   `/historical/`, `/archive/`, or camera-image-history paths on 2026-05-06.
   None located. FL511 is a real-time traveler-information portal, not a
   record system. No FDOT page describing historical camera image storage was
   identified.

2. **Annual rollup / intermediate archive paths.** No parallel image-history
   pipeline analogous to NDBC's monthly/annual spectral archive exists on
   Florida DOT infrastructure. FDOT publishes traffic count data and crash
   reports historically, but not camera image archives.

3. **Third-party archives.** No commercial vendor offers historical FL511
   camera images. INRIX, HERE, Geotab, Wejo sell derived speed/travel-time
   metrics, not raw historical image frames. No non-commercial archive
   identified.

4. **Wayback Machine coverage.** Same limitation as all DOT camera sister
   briefs — the Internet Archive does not capture binary image files served
   at rotating per-camera CDN URLs at minute resolution.

5. **Brief cadence requirement.** 5 captures/hr = approximately 12-minute
   resolution archive. No public source preserves FL511 camera images at
   this or any other cadence. A competitor in 2027 cannot reconstruct 2026
   Florida footage.

§5 hard-floor verdict: **passes.** Defensibility=10 stands.
