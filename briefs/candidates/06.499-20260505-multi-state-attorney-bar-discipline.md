## Discovery story

`multi_state_attorney_bar_discipline` was identified during the 2026-05-05
T1 discovery synthesis pass (iteration `20260505T150802Z-02beae`) as a
Lane-4+5 candidate explicitly diversifying the Lane-1-heavy diversification
target by applying the proven multi-state-medical-board (06.892) archetype
to legal regulation.

The pattern observation:

1. State bar associations across all 50 US states + DC + US territories
   (~53 jurisdictions) each publish attorney disciplinary actions on their
   own portals in their own formats.
2. Per-jurisdiction archives are durably preserved (Lane-1 thesis fails
   §5).
3. The closest aggregator (NLRDB) is statutorily restricted to bar
   regulators / courts; commercial competitors (Martindale-Hubbell, Avvo,
   Thomson Reuters Legal Tracker) cover attorney profile data at
   $500–$150k+/year price tiers but do NOT publish a structured cross-
   jurisdiction disciplinary corpus with explicit cross-jurisdiction entity
   resolution.
4. The defensible artifact is the structured + entity-resolved + ongoing
   cross-jurisdiction product; compute (per-state OCR/NER + cross-state
   attorney-identity canonicalization) is the barrier.

The buyer-pool observation distinguishes this from the medical-board
brief: physician credentialing is highly concentrated in hospital-
privileging units (~5,000 buyers each at $10k–$100k+/year), whereas
attorney vetting is fragmented across law firms (~50,000 US firms, varying
spend) + malpractice insurers (~6 major carriers, smaller per-attorney
spend than physician-malpractice) + state bars (53 budgets, modest each)
+ judicial commissions + legal research platforms + journalism. Score 7
(vs. medical-board's 9) reflects this honestly without inflating
defensibility.

## §5 Lane-4 verification

Per the cerebrum methodology, Lane-4 §5 verification requires the brief
to claim ≥2 of {compute-as-barrier, ongoing-update compounding, cross-
jurisdiction entity resolution}. This brief claims all three, with the
cross-jurisdiction entity resolution as a *delivered* v1 component (vs.
CSLB's roadmap):

1. **Compute-as-barrier — present and strong.** Per-jurisdiction
   onboarding (53 distinct portals + NY's 4 sub-portals = 57 effective
   sources, each with own format/quirks/parser logic) + OCR + NER +
   cross-jurisdiction entity resolution is genuinely 6–12 months of
   focused work for a competent solo operator. An analyst with the same
   raw HTML/PDFs needs that same investment to reproduce the corpus, and
   the result is a one-shot artifact, not a live ongoing-update product.
2. **Ongoing-update compounding — present and strong.** State bars
   publish disciplinary actions on rolling cadence (some monthly, some
   weekly, some per-action). A live captured + structured corpus is
   materially more valuable each month for buyers (current attorney risk
   profiles update continuously; historical depth compounds for
   longitudinal underwriting + outside-counsel-vetting models).
3. **Cross-jurisdiction entity resolution — present and v1-delivered.**
   The canonical attorney-identity layer (resolving a disbarred CA
   attorney to the same canonical record when admitted in TX or FL under
   a name variant) is the v1 product centerpiece. This is the
   distinguishing feature vs. Martindale-Hubbell / Avvo (single-state-
   profile) and Thomson Reuters Legal Tracker (research-tool, not a
   structured cross-jurisdiction entity-resolved disciplinary corpus).

§5 hard-floor verdict: **passes** (claims all 3, fully delivered in v1).
Defensibility = 7, not 0. Composite 6.499 stands.
