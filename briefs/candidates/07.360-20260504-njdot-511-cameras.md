## Discovery story

`njdot_511_cameras` was identified during the WISHLIST seeding pass on 2026-05-04
as the most direct extension of the `somd-cameras` archetype to a higher-commercial-
value geography.

The observation pattern is identical to how somd-cameras was originally noticed:

1. NJDOT publishes live camera images from 200+ cameras along the NJ Turnpike, GSP,
   and state highways via the publicly accessible 511nj.org portal.
2. NJDOT does not archive historical images — the portal is a real-time traffic
   operations tool, not a record system.
3. The NJ Turnpike and GSP corridor is materially more commercially important than
   the SOMD region: highest freight throughput in the Northeast, major distribution
   hubs for national logistics players, and the center of the mid-Atlantic commuter
   economy.

The additional observation that triggered the WISHLIST entry was the geographic
complementarity: the existing somd-cameras archive covers the Maryland end of the
I-95 corridor (Harry W. Nice Bridge, National Harbor, MD 5 / MD 235 / MD 4 / US 301).
Adding NJ cameras would create a continuous corridor archive from the DC suburbs to
the NYC metro area — a dataset whose value is greater than the sum of its parts for
buyers who care about I-95 as a system rather than a state-by-state view.

The brief was scored before a full camera URL endpoint verification was possible
(the 511nj.org API returns 403 without a browser Referer header). The proposed
capture architecture is proven by analogy to somd-cameras; the one pre-build step
not yet done is the browser devtools inspection to identify the camera image CDN URLs.

## Rubric calibration note

A composite of **7.360** places `njdot_511_cameras` above `somd-cameras` (7.221)
and below `ndbc_realtime_buoys` (7.600). This ordering is defensible:

- The NJ cameras score *higher* than somd-cameras on financial axis (6.833 vs. 6.667)
  due to denser geography and broader commercial buyer pool (NJ Turnpike freight vs.
  SOMD regional traffic). The defensibility-10 is the same in both.
- The NJ cameras score *lower* than ndbc_realtime_buoys on hardware axis (8.25 vs.
  9.25) because the storage footprint is 70× larger (70 GB/month vs. 1 GB/month) and
  the implementation is more complex (endpoint URL discovery, image roster management).
- The implementation axes are identical (7.25) — similar source stability and legal
  posture between NJDOT/somd-cameras patterns.

**Does combined MD+NJ I-95 coverage justify a higher financial score?**

The combined corridor argument is real but appropriately reflected in the current
scoring rather than artificially inflating any single sub-criterion:

- `buyer_existence: 7` (vs. somd's 6) captures the broader commercial buyer pool that
  the denser NJ geography enables, including corridor-level buyers who need both ends.
- `market_gap: 8` (same as somd) reflects that neither state archives its footage and
  no incumbent sells a combined I-95 archive — the gap is structural and identical in
  character to somd-cameras.
- The combination with somd-cameras does NOT add another point on any single axis
  because the combined-corridor value is a monetization *hypothesis*, not a proven
  buyer commitment. It is captured in the monetization hypotheses section instead.

If both archives are successfully built and a buyer emerges specifically for the
corridor product, a re-score should increase `buyer_existence` to 8 and
`market_gap` to 9 (proven combined buyer + demonstrated structural gap). That
would push financial to ~7.5 and composite toward ~7.8. Reserve that re-score for
when actual buyer interest or a comparable-corridor sale is in hand.

**No rubric changes recommended.** The formula correctly reflects that NJ cameras
are a stronger moat than SOMD cameras in financial terms (higher commercial density)
while being architecturally nearly identical. The gap to ndbc_realtime_buoys on
hardware is honest: 70 GB/month is real infrastructure cost vs. 1 GB/month for buoys.
