## Discovery story

`osha_enforcement_inspection_corpus` was identified during the 2026-05-05 T2
discovery synthesis pass to broaden the Lane-2 cluster (which had only one
prior entry, USDA APHIS Animal Welfare). Lane-2 ("soon-to-be-restricted data")
is structurally underrepresented because the political-vulnerability premise
requires both (a) a credible mechanism for restriction and (b) — ideally — a
documented prior restriction event.

OSHA satisfies (a) clearly: the current administration has visibly reduced
enforcement (inspection counts down, staffing/budget cuts in 2025, and
historical episodes of individual inspection-report suppression when challenged
by high-profile employers). It satisfies (b) only weakly — there is no
agency-wide database removal comparable to USDA's 2017 ACIS event — which is
why the brief honestly scores Defensibility lower than APHIS (5 vs. 7) and
positions the Lane-4 derived-artifact pillar as the structural fallback.

Two intentional defensive constructs:

1. The brief acknowledges data.gov bulk snapshots as the explicit §5 risk
   surface (rather than handwaving past it). The defensible delta narrows
   to (i) between-snapshot real-time capture, (ii) post-restriction
   durability, (iii) narrative-field preservation that bulk aggregations may
   strip — and the Defensibility=5 score reflects that narrowing honestly.

2. The Lane-4 secondary tag is a real fallback, not a checkbox. NLP-extracted
   violation narratives at scale + parent-company entity resolution satisfy
   2 of the 3 cerebrum Lane-4 pillars. The brief deliberately does NOT claim
   cross-jurisdiction ER (the third pillar) because OSHA is a single federal
   agency — that honesty is the difference between a 5 and a 7 on
   Defensibility.

## CONSTRAINTS §5 verification (Lane-2 conditional-moat reasoning, weaker variant)

The Lane-2 §5 reasoning here mirrors the APHIS structure but with explicit
acknowledgment of the weaker premise:

1. **Political-vulnerability premise — partial.** OSHA enforcement is
   visibly contracting (reduced inspections, staffing cuts) but no agency-wide
   database removal has occurred. The 2017 USDA ACIS withdrawal is the
   structural template for what a future OSHA restriction event would look
   like; whether one materializes is uncertain. Brief MUST flag if DOL
   announces institutional commitments binding successor administrations
   to permanent open public access, OR if a clear administrative decision
   to curtail public enforcement-data access actually occurs (which would
   strengthen the brief's value asymmetrically — calibration triggers should
   include re-scoring up if such an event happens during the brief lifetime).

2. **data.gov bulk substitution risk — explicit.** Unlike APHIS (where efile
   is the only public surface), OSHA inspection data is also republished on
   data.gov as bulk snapshots. The brief MUST verify at build phase that
   data.gov snapshots strip or summarize narrative-violation text — if they
   preserve full narrative detail at parity with data.dol.gov API, the
   defensible delta narrows further to the between-snapshot real-time
   capture window plus post-restriction durability, and Defensibility may
   need to drop to 3-4 (composite ~6.4). This is the brief's biggest open
   verification risk and is called out in the source_signals.

3. **Lane-4 derived-artifact pillar — partial fallback.** NLP-extracted
   violation narratives + parent-company ER are compute-as-barrier + ongoing-
   update compounding (2 of 3 cerebrum Lane-4 pillars). Cross-jurisdiction
   ER is structurally absent (single federal agency); brief explicitly does
   NOT claim it. If Lane-2 premise evaporates entirely, the brief survives
   in Lane 4 with re-scored Defensibility = 4 (composite ~6.4) — still
   positive, still in the cluster, but lower-priority than APHIS's
   stronger triple-pillar structure.

§5 hard floor: **passes** with explicit weaker-than-APHIS reasoning.
Defensibility = 5 (not 0). Composite 6.723 stands.
