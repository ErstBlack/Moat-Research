## Discovery story

`multi_state_ag_consumer_protection` was identified during the 2026-05-06 T1
discovery synthesis pass (iteration `20260506T172446Z-c69a3e`) as the natural
application of the multi-state Lane-4 enforcement corpus archetype to the
attorney general consumer protection domain — the broadest-scope enforcement
authority in the US with no existing structured cross-state public corpus.

The brief builds on the established pattern across the corpus: multi-state
medical board (06.892), multi-state pharmacy board (06.907), multi-state real
estate commission (06.914), and multi-state attorney bar (06.499) all apply
the same three-pillar Lane-4 structure (compute-as-barrier, ongoing-update
compounding, cross-jurisdiction entity resolution) to licensed professional
enforcement. AG consumer protection extends this pattern to corporate and
individual defendants across a broader enforcement scope — consumer fraud,
data privacy, antitrust, environmental, healthcare pricing, financial services
— with a distinct buyer pool (corporate compliance, D&O insurers, litigation
defense) rather than the credentialing-organization buyer pool of the licensing
corpora.

The key competitive distinction from the FTC brief (07.063) is scope and
structure: FTC is one federal agency; AG enforcement is 50 independent
jurisdictions each with broader state-law authority (state AG offices can
enforce laws that predate the FTC, including state consumer protection statutes,
state antitrust laws, state data privacy laws, state environmental laws). The
FTC brief derives its highest-value signal from the Lane-2 political-risk angle
(documented case withdrawals under the current administration); AG enforcement
lacks this specific Lane-2 angle (AG elections are decentralized, so political
vulnerability is diffuse rather than concentrated at one enforcement priority
decision-maker). This is why AG enforcement is Lane 4+5 rather than Lane 2+4+5.

The NAAG Consumer Protection Database (members-only, state regulatory agencies
only, no commercial tier) confirms the market gap: even the AGs' own trade
association does not provide a public structured cross-state enforcement corpus.
LexisNexis/Westlaw cover the raw documents but not the entity-resolved,
structured, continuously-updated cross-state corpus.

## §5 Lane-4 verification

Per the cerebrum CONSTRAINTS §5 verification methodology for Lane-4 briefs:

Raw state AG consumer protection enforcement records are publicly available
(each AG publishes on its own portal; LexisNexis/Westlaw have the raw
documents). This is EXPECTED for Lane 4: the raw is accessible; what matters
is whether the DERIVED ARTIFACT can be reconstructed later.

The three-pillar test per the cerebrum Lane-4 entity-resolution standard:

1. **Compute-as-barrier**: OCR (for PDF enforcement orders from many states)
   + NER (entity extraction: company names, individual names, violation
   categories, monetary relief, case numbers, violation statutes) + entity
   resolution (cross-state company identity canonicalization via legal name
   normalization, DBA/trade-name resolution, subsidiary mapping, state-of-
   incorporation cross-referencing). Reproducing this pipeline for all 50
   states and 12+ months of historical depth would take months of engineering
   time. The barrier is the accumulated corpus depth plus the ongoing
   maintenance overhead.

2. **Ongoing-update compounding**: State AGs publish new enforcement actions
   monthly to quarterly. A competitor starting in 2027 can build the same
   pipeline but cannot retroactively capture enforcement actions from 2026.
   The corpus's historical depth is the irreversible accumulation — especially
   valuable during periods of high-activity enforcement waves.

3. **Cross-state entity resolution as v1 deliverable**: A company like a
   national fintech operating as "FastCash LLC" in CA, "FastCash Financial
   Inc" in NY, and "FastCash Services Corp" in TX is the same defendant in
   all three states. Canonical entity resolution linking these records —
   surfacing the multi-state enforcement pattern as a unified defendant record
   — is the core v1 deliverable and requires the full multi-state corpus to
   compute. This is structurally equivalent to the medical board's FSMB-
   Federation thesis and the real estate board's cross-state license-number
   federation.

All three pillars satisfied. §5 hard-floor **passes** under Lane-4 framing.
Defensibility=7 stands.
