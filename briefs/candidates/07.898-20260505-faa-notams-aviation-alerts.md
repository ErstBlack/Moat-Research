description: |
  Continuously archive every NOTAM (Notice to Air Missions) issued in the FAA's
  flight information region — GPS-jamming/spoofing exercise corridors, runway
  closures, military-operations-area activations, VIP TFRs, drone restrictions,
  navaid outages, obstacle hazards, laser/light shows. Each NOTAM has a defined
  effective period; once it expires or is cancelled, the FAA NOTAM Search tool
  ceases to return it, and FAA's historical-NOTAM lookup (the only public
  retrospective channel) is partial, lags by months, and operational-detail
  fields can be redacted on retrospective release per FAA Order JO 7930.2. A
  continuous capture preserves full effective-period metadata, original
  operational-detail text, and cancellation timestamps that the live feed
  exposes but no public archive durably retains. The moat is the historical
  record: a 2026 OSINT analyst, a 2027 aviation-insurance underwriter, or a
  2028 academic-aviation-safety researcher cannot reconstruct the 2026
  GPS-denial-exercise footprint from the FAA's historical lookup at the
  fidelity the live feed exposed.

## Discovery story

`faa_notams_aviation_alerts` was identified during the 2026-05-05 discovery
synthesis pass (T2) explicitly to diversify lane coverage — the corpus had
zero Lane-2 and zero Lane-5 entries before T2, and only Lane 1 / Lane 4 active
candidates. NOTAMs are the textbook Lane-1 ephemeral source: a per-event
operational alert with a defined validity window, after which the live FAA
NOTAM Search tool ceases to return the record. The wishlist verification pass
established that:

1. The canonical public source (notams.aim.faa.gov/notamSearch/) is reachable
   without auth, has no robots.txt configured (HTTP 404 → default-allow per
   cerebrum's S3-precedent §2 reading), and is operational on a stable URL.
2. A separate registered-developer NOTAM REST API exists at external-api.faa.gov
   with free-token gating — per cerebrum's GTFS-RT precedent, free-token gating
   is not §1 auth-bypass and provides a structurally independent fallback path.
3. FAA's own historical-NOTAM lookup is partial AND operational-detail fields
   can be redacted on retrospective release per FAA Order JO 7930.2 — i.e., the
   live feed is materially richer than what the historical lookup returns
   months later. This is the §5 verification: the moat survives because
   retrospective fidelity is structurally degraded, not because retrospective
   access is absent.

The Lane-5 secondary tag (niche-vertical: aviation OSINT / safety /
drone-corridor planning) reflects that the highest-value buyers are not in the
mass commercial-aviation analytics market (where Cirium / FlightAware
dominate) but in narrower OSINT and insurance verticals where the operational-
detail fidelity gap is the value driver.

## CONSTRAINTS §5 verification (Lane-1 ephemeral-archive check)

Per the cerebrum's standard methodology for Lane-1 §5 verification:

1. **Upstream "historical data" / "archive" docs**: FAA's own historical-NOTAM
   lookup is the sole canonical retrospective channel. Documented at
   notams.aim.faa.gov/historical (UI subpath) and FAA Order JO 7930.2.
2. **Annual + intermediate archive paths**: NO complete annual rollup of
   expired NOTAMs at full-fidelity exists. The historical lookup is query-
   driven and retrospectively partial.
3. **Sample retrospective record column inspection**: a sample retrospective
   query for a known-expired NOTAM returns the structured fields (NOTAM_ID,
   ICAO_location, validity_window, type) but redacts operational-detail
   narrative for GPS-denial / SUA-activation / classified-TFR types per JO
   7930.2 §7-2 redaction discretion. The live-feed operational-detail is what
   our archive captures and the historical lookup does NOT preserve.
4. **Last-Modified freshness**: notamSearch UI bundle last-modified 2025-03-12;
   FAA infrastructure is stable.

Conclusion: Lane-1 ephemeral premise SURVIVES at the operational-detail-
fidelity level. Defensibility scored 9 (not 10) only because partial structured
retrospective signal exists in the FAA historical lookup and partial third-
party open archives (ICAO AIXM collectors, SkyVector, OpenAIP) — but the
brief's stated buyer use cases (OSINT, drone-corridor planning, aviation
insurance route-risk) require operational-detail fidelity that those partial
sources do not preserve. CONSTRAINTS §5 floor of `Defensibility = 0` is NOT
triggered.

Re-verification triggers: (a) FAA announces a complete operational-detail-
preserving retrospective archive, (b) a free third-party comprehensive
expired-NOTAM corpus emerges at our use-case fidelity, (c) FAA Order JO 7930.2
is amended to remove operational-detail redaction discretion on retrospective
release. In any of those cases, the brief must be re-scored or rejected.
