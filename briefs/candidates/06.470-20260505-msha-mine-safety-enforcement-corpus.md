## Discovery story

`msha_mine_safety_enforcement_corpus` was identified during the
2026-05-05 T2 second discovery synthesis pass to broaden the Lane-2
cluster from n=2 to n=5 alongside EPA ECHO and NLRB. MSHA satisfies
the Lane-2 capture-before-the-door-closes thesis with stronger
documented precedent than OSHA: the 2017–19 ~40% drop in MSHA
citation issuance is documented by DOL OIG audits with specific
quantified evidence, and FOIA stonewalling on Form 7000-50 fatal
accident investigation narratives during that era was a documented
investigative-journalism finding from mining-industry trade press.
The 2025 administration's signaled MSHA budget cuts and potential
consolidation of MSHA into a unified DOL safety agency extend the
political-vulnerability narrative.

The §5 reasoning is the structural mirror of OSHA / EPA ECHO with
mining-vertical specifics:

- **OGI bulk-table §5 risk surface — explicit and carved around.**
  arlweb.msha.gov's OGI portal provides full-historical-depth
  structured tables for Mines / Inspections / Violations /
  Accidents / Penalty Assessments. The brief MUST verify at build
  phase whether OGI tables include inspector narratives and Form
  7000-50 full text — they DO NOT (verified during T2 wishlist
  synthesis: OGI is summary-only, narratives live in citation PDFs
  and Form 7000-50 PDFs separately). The carved-out moat is the
  daily delta cadence + narrative-field preservation + Form 7000-50
  full-text durability.

- **Form 7000-50 as priority capture target.** The wishlist entry
  flags Form 7000-50 fatal accident investigation reports as
  historically the most-suppressed records under FOIA stonewalling.
  This is the brief's strongest Lane-2 defensible artifact — the
  category most likely to be restricted under a future MSHA
  reorganization or political pressure event.

- **Mining-vertical buyer-pool narrowing.** The brief honestly
  scores buyer-existence (5) and pricing-precedent (4) lower than
  OSHA / EPA ECHO / NLRB because the mining vertical is genuinely
  narrower with fewer comparable commercial vendors. This is
  reflected in the 6.470 composite, which is the lowest of the
  three new T2 Lane-2 entries.
