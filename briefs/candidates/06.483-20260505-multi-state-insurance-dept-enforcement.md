## Discovery story

`multi_state_insurance_dept_enforcement` was identified during the 2026-05-05
T2 discovery synthesis pass as a strong Lane-4 + Lane-5 candidate that
parallels the multi_state_medical_board_enforcement structural pattern
(cross-state portals + structured-extraction + cross-jurisdiction ER as v1
deliverable). The five-state pre-promotion verification on 2026-05-05
turned up three materially different friction modes — Missouri 429, WA
403, CA 404 — that medical-board's CA bellwether did not exhibit. This
pre-build signal is honestly priced into the implementation axis (source
stability 5 vs. medical-board's 6) and concurrency-cost score (7 vs. 8).

The Lane-4 thesis is unusually strong here for the same reasons it was
strong on medical-board: all three cerebrum defensibility pillars
(compute-as-barrier, ongoing-update compounding, cross-jurisdiction
entity resolution) are present in the v1 product, not deferred to a
roadmap as in CSLB. Cross-state insurance-licensee canonicalization
(producer name + license number + NAIC code + parent-company graph as
join keys) is itself a non-trivial moat layer.

The composite ordering vs. medical-board (6.483 vs. 6.892) is structural,
not arbitrary: medical-board has stronger financial axis (7.333 vs. 6.667
— hospital privileging is a larger and more concretely-priced market than
insurance-carrier compliance), better implementation (6.250 vs. 6.000 —
medical-board has cleaner per-portal verification baseline), and better
hardware (7.0 vs. 6.75 — verified per-portal friction observed in
insurance pre-build is an honest down-score). If both were built, medical-
board ships first as the higher-financial-ceiling Lane-4 candidate;
insurance is the parallel-pattern follow-on that benefits from the
canonicalization tooling already validated on medical-board.

## §5 Lane-4 verification

Per the cerebrum methodology, Lane-4 §5 verification requires the brief
to claim ≥2 of {compute-as-barrier, ongoing-update compounding, cross-
jurisdiction entity resolution}. This brief claims all three:

1. **Compute-as-barrier — present and strong.** Per-state onboarding
   (~55 jurisdictions × distinct portals, each with own format/quirks/
   parser logic) + OCR + NER + cross-state ER is genuinely 9–12 months
   of focused work. Pre-build verification confirmed three different
   per-portal friction modes (Missouri 429, WA 403, CA 404) in just
   five states checked, validating that the compute investment is real
   and not a paper claim.

2. **Ongoing-update compounding — present and strong.** State insurance
   departments publish enforcement orders on rolling cadence (some
   monthly, some weekly, some per-action). Live captured + structured
   corpus is materially more valuable each month for buyers (current
   licensee risk profiles update continuously; longitudinal depth
   compounds for underwriting and producer-vetting models).

3. **Cross-jurisdiction entity resolution — present and v1-delivered.**
   Resolving a national insurer's 50 state-specific subsidiaries to a
   single canonical parent-company entity is the v1 product centerpiece.
   This is the strongest cross-jurisdiction ER claim alongside medical-
   board (both are v1-delivered, not roadmap as in CSLB).

Free public alternatives (§5 reconstructibility check): NAIC publishes
summary actions only (verified 2026-05-05); no comprehensive structured
cross-state corpus exists publicly. Commercial paid alternatives
(Wolters Kluwer, IVANS, Verisk Sequel) do NOT trigger §5 per project
precedent (paid commercial barriers ≠ public reconstructibility — same
reading applied to Interline / Transitland for transit, S&P Velocity
Suite for FERC, and FSMB / NPDB for medical-board).

§5 hard floor: **passes** (claims all 3, fully delivered in v1, with
pre-build friction verification).
Defensibility = 7. Composite 6.483 stands.
