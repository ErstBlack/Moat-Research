## Discovery story

`cra_exam_narrative_corpus` was identified during the 2026-05-05 T3 Akamai-gated
endpoint alternate-path recheck pass. FFIEC (ffiec.gov) was confirmed Akamai-gated
with no alternate path through data.gov or FOIA reading rooms, but the 3-step
polite-alternate-path checklist found that individual member-regulator sites
(OCC: HTTP 200, Fed: HTTP 200) carry the same CRA exam PDFs directly. FFIEC is
a convenience aggregator, not the source — the actual exam publication obligation
falls to each member regulator under 12 U.S.C. § 2906(b).

The Lane-4 framing is clean: raw PDFs are publicly archived (Lane 1 fails §5),
but the structured narrative extraction corpus — with cross-regulator entity
resolution and normalized examiner-finding taxonomy — does not exist publicly at
any price point. Commercial CRA tools (S&P, Wolters Kluwer, Bloomberg) provide
case-tracking dashboards, not examiner-narrative taxonomy structured at the
finding level.

## CONSTRAINTS §5 verification (Lane-4 three-pillar test)

Lane 4 must demonstrate that the derived structured artifact passes §5 by showing
compute is the barrier (not data access):

1. **Compute-as-barrier.** OCR + NLP taxonomy extraction + cross-regulator entity
   resolution across three distinct regulatory exam frameworks (OCC 12 C.F.R. § 25,
   Fed § 228, FDIC § 345 — different schema, different rating scales, different
   exam scheduling triggers). An analyst with the same PDFs requires: (a) OCR
   pipeline for scanned exams (many pre-2015 exams are non-machine-readable PDFs),
   (b) domain-specific NLP taxonomy aligned to the four CRA evaluation categories
   plus sub-criteria (lending test is itself multi-component: penetration, geographic
   distribution, inside/outside assessment area, innovation, flexibility), (c) RSSD/
   NIC/FDIC cert → holding company → LEI entity resolution across three separate
   identifier namespaces. Month-scale effort minimum.

2. **Ongoing-update compounding.** ~1,000 new exams/year across three regulators,
   quarterly publication cadence. Corpus depth accumulates continuously; a 5-year
   longitudinal trajectory has materially more analytical value than a single-year
   snapshot, and that value is non-reconstructible retroactively once the narrative
   text extraction + peer-group normalization pipeline has been continuously applied.

3. **Cross-regulator entity resolution.** A bank holding company operating OCC-
   chartered national bank subsidiary + Fed-supervised state-member subsidiary +
   FDIC-supervised thrift requires ER across three separate identifier namespaces
   (RSSD for OCC/Fed, FDIC cert number, optional LEI). The consolidated holding-
   company CRA posture graph does not exist publicly; FFIEC's aggregate CRA ratings
   table provides institution-level ratings without entity graph.

§5 hard floor: **passes**. Defensibility = 7 (not 0). Lane-4 three-pillar test
satisfied in full. Composite 7.274 stands.
