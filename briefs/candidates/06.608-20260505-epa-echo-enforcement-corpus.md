## Discovery story

`epa_echo_enforcement_corpus` was identified during the 2026-05-05 T2
second discovery synthesis pass to broaden the Lane-2 cluster from n=2 to
n=5 alongside MSHA and NLRB. EPA ECHO satisfies the Lane-2
capture-before-the-door-closes thesis with stronger documented precedent
than OSHA: the 2017–18 EPA OECA homepage scrubs (cumulative civil penalty
totals dashboard, climate-change pages, OECA enforcement annual results
page) are SPECIFIC documented prior events of discoverability-layer
restriction, archived by the Environmental Data & Governance Initiative
(EDGI). The 2025 OECA budget cuts and reduced enforcement posture
extend the political-vulnerability narrative to the present.

The §5 reasoning is structurally distinct from OSHA's data.gov-bulk-
substitution concern:

- **Bulk-dump vs. discoverability-layer distinction.** EPA's bulk dumps
  (ICIS-AIR, ICIS-NPDES, RCRAInfo, SDWIS) ARE mirrored on data.gov AND
  are durably archived even through the 2017 OECA scrubs (the scrubs
  targeted aggregate dashboards and homepage discoverability, not the
  raw bulk dumps). The Lane-2 moat is therefore carved out away from the
  bulk-dump layer entirely: weekly-delta capture between bulk releases,
  case-narrative field preservation, per-DMR per-parameter detail, and
  discoverability-layer durability are the carved-out moat surface.

- **EDGI as forensic precedent reference.** Unlike OSHA (where the
  Lane-2 vulnerability is asserted but not yet documented), ECHO has
  EDGI's 2017–18 archival project as the SPECIFIC forensic precedent —
  Wayback Machine captures of removed OECA pages provide direct evidence
  of the political-restriction event geometry that the Lane-2 moat
  positions against.

   Defensibility drops to 4 (composite ~6.3) and the brief should be
