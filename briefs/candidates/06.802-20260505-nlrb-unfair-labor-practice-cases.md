## Discovery story

`nlrb_unfair_labor_practice_cases` was identified during the 2026-05-05 T2
second discovery synthesis pass to broaden the Lane-2 cluster from n=2 to n=5
alongside EPA ECHO and MSHA. NLRB satisfies the Lane-2 capture-before-the-
door-closes thesis with stronger documented precedent than OSHA: the 2017
Robb-era General Counsel publication-restriction memos are a specific event
where regional case detail was withdrawn from publication, and the 2025
Wilcox v. Trump quorum-collapse directly disrupted case adjudication and
publication for several months.

The §5 reasoning is the most carefully carved out of the three Lane-2
candidates this pass:

- **Cornell LII §5 risk surface, explicit.** Cornell Legal Information
  Institute publicly mirrors NLRB Board decisions. This is NOT a paid
  commercial competitor (which would be exempt from §5 per project
  precedent on Interline / S&P Velocity Suite); it is a free public
  archive that potentially triggers §5 for the Board-decisions slice
  specifically. The brief's moat is therefore carved AROUND the Cornell
  LII coverage: case-tracker / regional charge detail, settlement-term
  extraction, GC publication-policy timeline, and withdrawal narratives
  are explicitly outside Cornell LII's coverage and ARE the politically-
  sensitive detail layer that Robb-era memos targeted.

- **Per-region detail layer is the moat.** The Lane-2 detail-layer
  carve-out reasoning codified in cerebrum (2026-05-05 T2) applies
  directly: bulk archives + Cornell LII cover the decisions and
  high-level dispositions; the case-tracker XHR endpoint surfaces
  per-region case detail (settlement memos, withdrawal letters,
  GC-region OM memos) that has NEVER been mirrored at parity by
  Cornell LII or any commercial archive.
