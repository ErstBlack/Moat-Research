## Discovery story

`multi_state_medical_board_enforcement` was identified during the 2026-05-04
discovery synthesis pass as a strong Lane-4 candidate where (a) the raw per-state
enforcement actions are publicly archived (failing any Lane-1 thesis under §5),
but (b) no public structured cross-state corpus with entity-resolved physician
records exists. The two existing closest analogues — FSMB Physician Data Center
(paid, restricted to credentialing orgs) and NPDB (statutorily restricted to
hospitals/insurers) — confirm both the commercial value of the cross-state
aggregation and the structural gap in the public market.

The Lane-4 thesis is unusually strong here because all three defensibility
pillars (compute-as-barrier, ongoing-update compounding, cross-jurisdiction
entity resolution) are present in the v1 product, not deferred to a roadmap as
in the CSLB brief. Cross-state physician identity canonicalization (name + DOB
+ medical-school + specialty as join keys, with manual disambiguation for low-
confidence matches) is itself a non-trivial moat layer that any analyst trying
to reproduce the corpus would have to solve from scratch.

The financial axis (7.333, highest of the new candidates) reflects the unusual
combination of high buyer-existence (9), strong pricing precedent (8 — NPDB,
FSMB, hospital credentialing platforms), strong ongoing-revenue compounding
(9), and a structurally durable market gap (7 — FSMB's consortium model is not
positioned to serve the broader market this brief targets).

## §5 Lane-4 verification

Per the cerebrum methodology, Lane-4 §5 verification requires the brief to
claim ≥2 of {compute-as-barrier, ongoing-update compounding, cross-jurisdiction
entity resolution}. This brief claims all three, with the cross-jurisdiction
entity resolution as a *delivered* v1 component (vs. CSLB's roadmap):

1. **Compute-as-barrier — present and strong.** Per-state onboarding (50
   distinct portals, each with own format/quirks/parser logic) + OCR + NER +
   cross-state entity resolution is genuinely 6–12 months of focused work for
   a competent solo operator. An analyst with the same raw HTML/PDFs needs
   that same investment to reproduce the corpus, and the result is a one-shot
   artifact, not a live ongoing-update product.
2. **Ongoing-update compounding — present and strong.** State medical boards
   publish enforcement actions on rolling cadence (some monthly, some weekly,
   some per-action). A live captured + structured corpus is materially more
   valuable each month for buyers (current physician risk profiles update
   continuously; historical depth compounds for longitudinal underwriting
   models).
3. **Cross-jurisdiction entity resolution — present and v1-delivered.** The
   canonical physician-identity layer (resolving "Dr. John Smith" in CA / NY /
   FL to a single canonical record) is the v1 product centerpiece, not a
   roadmap. This is the strongest cross-jurisdiction ER claim of the new
   Lane-4 candidates.

§5 hard-floor verdict: **passes** (claims all 3, fully delivered in v1).
Defensibility = 7, not 0. Composite 6.892 stands.
