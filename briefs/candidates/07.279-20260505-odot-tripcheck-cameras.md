## Discovery story

`odot_tripcheck_cameras` was identified during the 2026-05-05 T1 discovery
synthesis pass (iteration `20260505T163223Z-a75c26`) as a Lane-1 candidate
closing the Pacific Coast DOT-camera corridor gap between Caltrans CA and
WSDOT WA. The brief explicitly fills three coverage gaps not addressed by
any existing camera entry:

1. **I-84 Columbia River Gorge** — the only practical east-west freight
   route through the Pacific Northwest. Neither Caltrans (southern) nor
   WSDOT (northern I-90/SR-520) covers this east-west spine. A camera
   archive over the Gorge captures freight flow timing from Portland's
   port complex and Willamette Valley DCs to the entire inland West
   (Boise ID, Twin Falls, Pocatello, Salt Lake City UT, Denver CO).

2. **Intel Hillsboro campus and Portland metro distribution** — Intel's
   Ronler Acres fab in Hillsboro is the company's largest US semiconductor
   manufacturing site; its supply-chain logistics team monitors US-26 /
   NW Evergreen access corridor congestion for just-in-time wafer delivery
   and outbound finished-chip shipment. Nike HQ (Beaverton) and Adidas NA
   (Portland) add corporate-campus logistics buyers distinct from the
   Amazon/Microsoft buyer base in WA.

3. **Cascade pass closures (US-26/US-20/US-97)** — Oregon's Cascade
   crossings differ from WSDOT's I-90 Snoqualmie: lower elevation,
   different closure pattern, and connecting different agricultural origins
   (Willamette Valley / Rogue Valley wine, hazelnuts, hops vs. eastern WA
   apples, wheat). A separate pass-closure archive for Oregon-specific
   agricultural cargo has a distinct buyer in specialty-crop insurance.

The open `/api/cameras` endpoint (HTTP 200, no auth, returns camera
inventory with image URL metadata) distinguishes ODOT from WSDOT, which
required a free developer-token registration. This makes the Oregon ingestor
simpler and removes any developer-agreement ToS review step for image access.

## §5 Lane-1 verification

Per the cerebrum CONSTRAINTS §5 verification methodology:

1. **Upstream archive paths.** Checked tripcheck.com and odot.oregon.gov
   for any `/historical/`, `/archive/`, or image-history paths on 2026-05-05.
   None located. ODOT's TripCheck is explicitly a real-time traveler-
   information portal, not a record system. The `/api/cameras` endpoint
   returns current camera metadata + live image URLs, with no historical
   endpoint documented.

2. **Annual rollup / intermediate paths.** No parallel image-history
   pipeline analogous to NDBC's monthly/annual spectral archive exists on
   Oregon DOT infrastructure.

3. **Third-party archives.** No commercial vendor offers historical ODOT
   TripCheck camera images. INRIX, HERE, Geotab, and traffic analytics
   platforms sell derived speed/travel-time metrics, not raw historical
   image frames. Wayback Machine does not archive binary image files served
   at rotating per-camera URLs at any meaningful cadence.

4. **Wayback Machine coverage.** Identical limitation as all DOT camera
   sister briefs — the Internet Archive's web-crawler infrastructure does
   not capture image CDN endpoints at minute resolution.

5. **Brief cadence requirement.** Targeting ~5-minute polling per camera
   (minute-resolution archive). No public source preserves images at this
   cadence; a competitor in 2027 cannot reconstruct 2026 Oregon footage.

§5 hard-floor verdict: **passes.** Defensibility=10 stands.
