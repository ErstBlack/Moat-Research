## Discovery story

`usda_aphis_animal_welfare_inspections` was identified during the 2026-05-05
discovery synthesis pass (T2) explicitly to satisfy the lane-diversification
acceptance criterion — the corpus had zero Lane-2 candidates active before T2.
Lane-2 ("soon-to-be-restricted data") is structurally underrepresented because
the political-vulnerability premise requires both (a) a documented prior
restriction event and (b) a credible mechanism for re-restriction; most
candidates encountered during routine discovery either lack precedent or are
already permanently restricted.

The USDA APHIS Animal Care corpus has a well-documented precedent: in February
2017 USDA abruptly removed the public Animal Care Information System database;
HSUS v. Vilsack et al. and parallel FOIA litigation produced years of partial
restoration; the current efile public search tool replaced ACIS in ~2020. The
re-restriction mechanism is concrete: shifting administrations have, within
recent memory, treated the public-access posture as discretionary. This is the
clearest available Lane-2 thesis the discovery pass surfaced.

The brief intentionally combines Lane 2 (capture-before-the-door-closes) with
Lane 4 (compute-as-barrier structured extraction + cross-licensee parent-
company entity resolution) and Lane 5 (niche-vertical: animal-welfare
compliance / lab-animal welfare / pet-trade enforcement) so that the
defensibility argument has multiple independent pillars: even if the political-
vulnerability premise weakens (e.g., USDA institutionalizes permanent public
access), the Lane-4 derived-artifact moat survives.

## CONSTRAINTS §5 verification (Lane-2 conditional-moat reasoning)

The Lane-1 §5 verification methodology in cerebrum is "is the raw archive
reconstructible from currently-public sources at the cadence the brief
depends on?" — applied to this brief naively, that test FAILS, because efile
currently provides public access to inspection reports.

But that is the wrong test for Lane 2. The Lane-2 moat is structurally
DIFFERENT: the brief is not promising "an analyst could not currently
reconstruct this archive" — efile currently allows that. The brief is
promising "an analyst attempting reconstruction AFTER a future restriction
event will face FOIA-litigation-paced partial recovery (precedent: HSUS v.
Vilsack et al. produced years of partial restoration)." The moat is the
asymmetric difficulty of capture-now vs. reconstruct-later, not the
impossibility of capture-now.

Two §5-style checks therefore apply:

1. **Political-vulnerability premise is real**: the 2017 ACIS withdrawal is
   the documented precedent; HSUS v. Vilsack et al. and parallel FOIA
   litigation establish that re-restriction is mechanically possible and
   has happened within recent memory. Brief MUST flag if USDA announces
   an institutional commitment to permanent open public access binding
   successor administrations — that would weaken the conditional moat.

2. **Lane-4 derived-artifact pillar is independently defensible**: even if
   the political-vulnerability premise weakens, the cross-licensee parent-
   company entity resolution + NLP-extracted citation-narrative corpus at
   scale satisfies the cerebrum's "Lane 4 entity-resolution as defensibility"
   methodology (compute-as-barrier + ongoing-update compounding + cross-
   licensee ER). This is the corresponding-Lane-4 sanity check: if the
   Lane-2 premise evaporates, the brief survives in Lane 4 (with re-scored
   defensibility — recommended re-score Defensibility = 6 in that scenario,
   composite drops by ~0.15, still positive).

Brief therefore passes §5 with explicit dual-pillar reasoning. CONSTRAINTS §5
hard floor of `Defensibility = 0` is NOT triggered.
