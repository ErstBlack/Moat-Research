## Discovery story

`multi_state_real_estate_commission_enforcement` was identified during the
2026-05-06 T1 discovery synthesis pass (iteration `20260506T134246Z-edfe37`)
as the natural application of the multi-state professional-licensing-enforcement
archetype (established in the corpus via medical board at 06.892, pharmacy board
at 06.907, attorney bar at 06.499) to the real estate industry — the largest
licensed-professional category by headcount (~2 million active licensees in the US)
without an existing cross-state enforcement corpus.

The archetype is well-established: per-state enforcement portals publish public
records; no cross-state aggregator offers commercial access; compute (OCR + NER +
entity resolution) is the barrier that creates the moat. Each iteration of the
pattern has added a different buyer pool and competitive landscape analysis. Real
estate brings title insurance (a multi-billion-dollar industry with direct agent-
fraud exposure) and mortgage lenders (regulatory credentialing requirements) as
primary buyers — different from but analogous in motivation to the PBM-credentialing
buyers that anchor the pharmacy board brief.

The key competitive distinction from medical/pharmacy boards is the ARELLO presence.
Unlike the medical board (FSMB Physician Data Center is restricted to credentialing
organizations at per-query pricing) or pharmacy board (NABP e-Profile restricted to
licensed requestors), ARELLO's discipline database is accessible to state regulatory
agencies but not to commercial buyers. This creates a structurally similar market
gap, with the caveat that ARELLO could theoretically open a commercial tier without
needing to build new infrastructure — reducing the moat's permanence slightly vs.
the healthcare-board landscape.

The TX TREC enforcement URL (404 on tested path on 2026-05-06) is a build-phase
concern, not a dismissal trigger. TREC operates on Drupal CMS, which periodically
restructures enforcement-listing paths; the main domain is healthy and TREC
publishes enforcement orders publicly by statute.

## §5 Lane-4 verification

Per the cerebrum CONSTRAINTS §5 verification methodology for Lane-4 briefs:

The raw state real estate commission enforcement records are publicly available
(each state publishes its own orders on its own portal). This is EXPECTED for
Lane 4: the raw is publicly archived; what matters is whether the DERIVED ARTIFACT
can be reconstructed later.

The three-pillar test per the cerebrum Lane-4 entity-resolution standard:

1. **Compute-as-barrier**: OCR (for PDF orders from the majority of states) + NER
   (entity extraction: names, license numbers, violation types, case numbers) +
   entity resolution (cross-state agent identity canonicalization via name
   normalization + license-number federation + address matching). Reproducing
   this pipeline for 50 states and 6-12 months of historical depth would take
   months of engineering time for a competitor. The barrier is the accumulated
   corpus depth plus the ongoing maintenance overhead — not the data itself.

2. **Ongoing-update compounding**: State commissions publish new enforcement orders
   monthly to quarterly. A competitor starting in 2027 can build the same pipeline
   but cannot retroactively capture the 2026 enforcement actions that occurred
   before they began. The corpus's historical depth (which agents were disciplined
   in which states starting when) is the irreversible accumulation.

3. **Cross-jurisdiction ER as v1 deliverable**: Agents licensed in multiple states
   (border-market areas: AZ/NV/CA multi-state licensing, NC/SC/TN/GA automotive-
   corridor markets, MD/DC/VA metro, FL/GA/TN border markets) hold different
   state-assigned license numbers in each state. Canonical entity resolution linking
   these records — "Agent X holds CA license #1234, AZ license #5678, NV license
   #9012, and has a pending suspension in AZ" — is the core v1 deliverable and
   requires the full multi-state corpus to compute. This is structurally
   equivalent to the medical board's FSMB-Federation thesis.

All three pillars satisfied. §5 hard-floor **passes** under Lane-4 framing.
Defensibility=7 stands (all three pillars present, slightly discounted from
medical board's 7 by ARELLO's institutional proximity to the commercial market).
