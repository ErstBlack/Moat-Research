## Discovery story

`caltrans_quickmap_cameras` was identified during the 2026-05-05 T1 discovery
synthesis pass (iteration `20260505T150802Z-02beae`) as a Lane-1 candidate
explicitly diversifying the lane cluster away from L2 saturation (9 entries
after the prior iteration) and extending the proven somd / NJDOT / TxDOT
camera archetype to the most populous US state and the Pacific freight
gateway.

The pattern observation is identical to how the prior camera briefs were
spotted:

1. Caltrans publishes live camera images from ~2,200 cameras across CA state
   highways and interstates via the publicly accessible QuickMap portal
   (quickmap.dot.ca.gov) backed by CWWP2 (cwwp2.dot.ca.gov).
2. Caltrans does not archive historical images — QuickMap is a real-time
   traffic situational-awareness tool, not a record system.
3. California commercial corridors are distinct from the East Coast and
   Texas: LA/Long Beach + Oakland are the dominant US container ports; the
   Bay Area tech corridor has its own logistics buyer base; the Central
   Valley is the top US agricultural producer; and the Sierra Nevada
   mountain passes are critical for trans-state freight with winter closure
   risk.

The buyer-mix angle is the brief's distinctive financial-axis claim: the
combined Pacific gateway + tech corridor + agricultural corridor + cross-
border (Tijuana) buyer pool is materially broader than any prior single-
state camera brief. The combined national archive (somd + NJDOT + TxDOT +
Caltrans) closes the major remaining gap in a national US freight-corridor
camera dataset.

## §5 Lane-1 verification

Per the cerebrum CONSTRAINTS §5 verification methodology, Lane-1
verification requires checking that the source is not archived by the
publisher or any third party at the cadence the brief depends on:

1. **Upstream archive paths.** Checked quickmap.dot.ca.gov,
   cwwp2.dot.ca.gov, and www.dot.ca.gov for any `/historical/`, `/archive/`,
   or similar paths on 2026-05-05. None located. Caltrans' California Open
   Data Portal (data.ca.gov) indexes some Caltrans datasets (PeMS sensor
   data, AADT, project tracking) but does NOT include historical camera
   image archives.
2. **Annual rollup / intermediate paths.** No equivalent of NDBC's
   `/data/swden/` monthly+annual rollup pattern exists on Caltrans
   infrastructure. CWWP2 is the live-image-serving layer only; no parallel
   image-history pipeline.
3. **Third-party archives.** No commercial vendor offers historical
   Caltrans camera images (verified by reviewing the traffic-data analytics
   market on 2026-05-05; INRIX, HERE, Geotab, Wejo, PortVision, Project44
   all sell aggregated derived metrics from camera + sensor input but not
   raw historical image frames; PortVision/Project44 cover vessel AIS not
   landside camera).
4. **Wayback Machine coverage.** Wayback does not archive binary image
   files served at rotating per-camera URLs at any meaningful cadence; this
   is a well-established limitation of the Internet Archive's web-crawler
   infrastructure for image CDNs.
5. **Brief cadence requirement.** The brief targets minute-resolution
   capture (~5-minute polling per camera). No public source preserves
   images at this cadence; a competitor in 2027 cannot reconstruct 2026
   frames at any price.

§5 hard-floor verdict: **passes.** Defensibility=10 stands; the past is
structurally unrecoverable for any party that didn't capture it in
real-time.
