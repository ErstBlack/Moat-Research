## Discovery story

`adot_az511_cameras` was identified during the 2026-05-06 T1 discovery synthesis
pass (iteration `20260506T172446Z-c69a3e`) as the highest-priority geographic gap
in the DOT-camera corpus after the GDOT brief (07.141) closed the Southeast
inland gap. The existing corpus now covers: Pacific Coast (Caltrans CA 07.139,
WSDOT WA 06.997, ODOT OR 07.279), Southwest/Texas interior (TxDOT TX 07.216),
Southeast (somd MD/VA 07.221, NJDOT NJ 07.360, FL511 FL 07.283, GDOT GA 07.141).
The I-10 corridor from El Paso west through Tucson and Phoenix to Los Angeles
remained uncovered; specifically, the Nogales POE approach corridors (I-19,
southern I-10) were the most commercially distinct uncovered segment in the
full US highway network.

The Nogales POE cross-border supply chain angle is entirely distinct from all
existing camera briefs. No other brief covers the primary US land gateway for
US-Mexico goods flows. The Nogales POE processes ~$30B/year in bilateral trade,
makes it the #2 US commercial land crossing by truck volume (after Laredo, covered
by TxDOT), and the #1 US fresh produce port of entry by value — fresh vegetables
(tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers) from Mexico's Sonora and Sinaloa states enter the
US food supply via this single checkpoint every winter/spring season.

The Hermosillo automotive corridor (Ford/VISTEON Hermosillo — the largest Ford
plant globally by vehicle output, ~400K/year) represents a distinct buyer vertical
from any existing brief. I-19 south of Tucson is the only northbound overland path
for this plant's output and inbound parts, creating a compelling JIT supply-chain
intelligence product for OEM logistics teams.

The robots.txt selectively blocks the /list/GetData/ camera metadata API endpoint,
requiring browser-devtools URL-template discovery for image capture — same
methodology used successfully for GDOT (NaviTech/SilverLogic platform) and WSDOT.
This is the standard approach for DOT-camera corpus projects and does not affect
image CDN accessibility.

## Rubric calibration note

Composite **7.138** places `adot_az511_cameras` marginally below GDOT (7.141),
reflecting nearly identical mechanics with one scoring differentiator:

- **Effectively equal to GDOT (7.141, Δ=0.003):** Both score financial=6.833,
  implementation=6.750, hardware=8.000. The Δ=0.003 is arithmetic rounding and
  does not represent a meaningful quality distinction. ADOT and GDOT serve
  entirely non-overlapping geographic markets: GDOT covers the SE seaboard freight
  and Port of Savannah; ADOT covers the US-Mexico border POE and Hermosillo
  automotive corridor.

- **Below FL511 (7.283):** FL511 benefits from a multi-seaport angle (three SE
  ports), better-documented iCone platform (source_stability=7 vs. ADOT's 6),
  and lower effective concurrency cost.

- **Above WSDOT (6.997):** ADOT's Nogales cross-border buyer vertical is more
  concentrated and commercially motivated than WSDOT's Puget Sound/tech-campus
  buyer pool.

- **Below TxDOT (7.216):** TxDOT covers Laredo (the #1 US commercial land
  crossing by truck volume vs. Nogales at #2), I-35 energy corridor, and I-10
  Houston Port access — a larger combined buyer pool than ADOT's AZ coverage.

The Δ=0.003 difference from GDOT accurately reflects defensible trade-off
equivalence under the multiplicative formula. No rubric changes recommended.

## §5 Lane-1 verification

Per the cerebrum CONSTRAINTS §5 verification methodology:

1. **Upstream archive paths.** Checked az511.gov and ADOT parent domains for
   any `/historical/`, `/archive/`, or camera-image-history paths on 2026-05-06.
   None located. AZ511 is a real-time traveler-information portal; ADOT publishes
   no historical camera image archive.

2. **Annual rollup / intermediate archive paths.** No parallel image-history
   pipeline exists on Arizona DOT infrastructure. ADOT publishes traffic count
   data and crash statistics historically, but not camera image archives.

3. **Third-party archives.** No commercial or non-commercial vendor offers
   historical AZ511 camera images. INRIX, HERE, StreetLight Data sell derived
   speed/travel-time metrics, not raw historical image frames. No non-commercial
   archive identified.

4. **Wayback Machine coverage.** Same limitation as all DOT camera sister
   briefs — the Internet Archive does not capture binary image files served
   at rotating per-camera CDN URLs at minute resolution.

5. **Brief cadence requirement.** ~1-minute capture cadence = ~60 frames/hr/camera.
   No public source preserves AZ511 camera images at this or any other cadence.
   A competitor in 2027 cannot reconstruct 2026 Arizona footage.

§5 hard-floor verdict: **passes.** Defensibility=10 stands.
