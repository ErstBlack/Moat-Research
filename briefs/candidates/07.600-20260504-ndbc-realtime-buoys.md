## Discovery story

`ndbc_realtime_buoys` was identified by the operator during the initial WISHLIST
seeding pass on 2026-05-04 as the first non-cameras candidate in Lane 1. The
observation pattern mirrors `somd-cameras` almost exactly:

1. NOAA NDBC publishes high-quality real-time oceanographic data from ~1,000
   buoys globally, updated every 10-50 minutes, via a publicly accessible HTTP
   directory at `https://www.ndbc.noaa.gov/data/realtime2/`.
2. The spectral wave density files (w-files, e.g. `41025w.txt`) report wave
   height broken out by frequency bin -- the kind of data that matters for
   offshore operations planning and marine insurance -- but each file is
   **overwritten** as new observations arrive.
3. NCEI appears to archive the primary hourly-aggregated observations but NOT
   the full-resolution spectral data at sub-hourly cadence. If accurate, this
   means every 10-50 minute spectral reading that passes without capture is
   gone permanently.

The moat candidate was added to `WISHLIST.md` with a flag: "Brief must verify
NCEI archive completeness for spectral (w-file) data before asserting Lane 1 --
if NCEI archives spectral files fully, the ephemeral argument weakens." That
flag is reflected in `disqualifiers_checked.ncei_spectral_archival_unverified`
and drives the conservative scores on `market_gap` (6) and `defensibility` (9
rather than 10).

The hardware and implementation profile is substantially better than
`somd-cameras`: 1 GB/month vs. 90 GB/month storage, no GPU required, no
HLS/video complexity, no inference pipeline. The operational footprint is about
as small as a moat project can get.

## Rubric calibration note

A composite of **7.600** places `ndbc_realtime_buoys` modestly above
`somd-cameras` (7.221) despite the lane-1 premise being unverified. This
feels like the right ordering: the hardware and implementation profiles are
clearly superior (9.25 and 8.25 vs. 8.0 and 7.25), and those two axes pull
the composite up even though the financial axis is nearly identical (6.167
vs. 6.667 -- slightly lower here because of the NCEI uncertainty discount on
`market_gap`).

The composite would increase substantially if the NCEI spectral gap is
confirmed: `market_gap` could move from 6 to 8, `defensibility` from 9 to 10,
pushing `financial` to ~7.0 and the composite toward ~8.1. Conversely, if
NCEI archives w-files fully, this becomes a Lane 3 brief; the defensibility
score drops, financial likely falls to 5.5-6.0, and the composite would
settle around 6.5-7.0 (still viable, but weaker).

**Action item for next iteration:** Verify NCEI spectral (w-file) archive
completeness at `https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/access/search/data-search/global-marine`
before promoting this brief to `scored`. If gap confirmed, update scores
accordingly and flip `ncei_spectral_archival_unverified` to `false`. If gap
absent, reclassify to Lane 3 and re-score.

**No rubric changes recommended.** The formula is working correctly: it
correctly discounts an unverified lane-1 claim through conservative
`market_gap` scoring rather than through rubric tinkering. The
implementation and hardware axes validating the operational feasibility
independently of the lane-1 premise gives a reliable floor score.
