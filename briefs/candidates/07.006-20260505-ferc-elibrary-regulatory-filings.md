## CONSTRAINTS §5 verification (Lane-4 derived-artifact reasoning)

The Lane-1 §5 verification methodology (raw-archive reconstructibility) FAILS
trivially for this brief: FERC durably archives all eLibrary filings AND
publishes bulk Form 1/6/714/EQR CSVs. A thesis claiming "duplicate FERC's
public archive" or "duplicate Form 1 CSVs" would auto-reject under §5.

Lane-4 §5 reasoning is structurally different. Per the cerebrum's "Lane 4
entity-resolution as defensibility" methodology, a Lane-4 brief satisfying §5
must articulate three pillars. This brief explicitly satisfies all three:

1. **Compute-as-barrier**: 10M+ filings × OCR + NER + entity-resolution at
   scale = months-to-years for an analyst with the same public PDFs to
   reproduce. Narrative-attachment NLP over multi-decade hydropower
   environmental-impact analyses, rate-case settlement explanations, and Form
   1 narrative schedules requires substantial pipeline engineering. The
   transformation from raw PDF + bulk-CSV inputs to docket-graph + canonical-
   parent-company + narrative-corpus outputs is the moat.

2. **Ongoing-update compounding**: FERC filings arrive on a continuous weekly
   cadence (rate cases, Order 1920 compliance filings, Order 2222 DER
   tariffs, certificate applications, settlement filings). Each new filing
   extends the docket-graph and updates parent-company resolutions. The
   corpus's current value depends on continuous capture, not a one-time pass.

3. **Cross-filing entity resolution**: subsidiary LLC → parent utility
   holding-company canonicalization across the ~3,000 FERC-jurisdictional-
   entity population is non-trivial. Utility restructurings, mergers, and
   subsidiary name changes happen continuously; FERC's filer-by-filer indexing
   does NOT roll up to holding-company level natively. This is the third
   pillar of moat defensibility.

All three pillars present → Lane-4 §5 floor of `Defensibility = 0` does NOT
trigger. Defensibility scored 7 (not 9–10) honestly discounts for the fact
that well-resourced incumbents (S&P Velocity Suite, ICF, FERC-specialty law
firms) have the capital to close any one pillar individually. They have not
because their commercial flagships are schedule-analytics-shaped, not docket-
graph + narrative-NLP-shaped — but the structural permanence of that gap is
weaker than Lane-1 unrecoverability.

Re-verification triggers: (a) FERC publishes a complete narrative-attachment
extraction corpus or docket-graph layer; (b) S&P Velocity Suite or ICF
announces a comparable docket-graph + narrative-NLP product; (c) a free open
structured corpus emerges (academic-press initiative or open-data
collaborative); (d) any single one of the three Lane-4 pillars is
structurally closed by an incumbent. In any of those cases, the brief must be
re-scored or rejected per the operator's no-moat=no-keep policy.

CEII (Critical Energy Infrastructure Information) protection is an EXCLUSION
from this brief's scope, not an obstacle: the capture pipeline respects FERC's
public search-tool CEII filter and NEVER attempts to access CEII-protected
filings through any other path. Bypassing CEII would be §1 (auth-bypass)
territory and is hard-disqualified.
