## Discovery story

`txdot_drivetexas_cameras` was identified during the 2026-05-05 T2 discovery
synthesis pass as a Lane-1 candidate explicitly diversifying the lane cluster
away from L2 saturation (5 entries) and extending the proven somd / NJDOT
camera archetype to the highest-freight-volume state in the US by lane-mile.

The pattern observation is identical to how the prior camera briefs were
spotted:

1. TxDOT publishes live camera images from ~1,000 cameras across Texas state
   highways and interstates via the publicly accessible drivetexas.org portal.
2. TxDOT does not archive historical images — the portal is a real-time
   traffic operations tool, not a record system.
3. Texas commercial corridors are distinct from the Northeast: I-35 Laredo
   carries the #1 US-Mexico port-of-entry trade value, I-10/I-20/I-37 carry
   the petrochemical and oilfield logistics flow, and combined coverage with
   somd / NJDOT closes the major remaining gap in a multi-corridor freight
   dataset.

The buyer-mix angle is the brief's distinctive financial-axis claim: where
the somd / NJDOT briefs lean on Northeast accident-reconstruction litigation
and commuter analytics, the TxDOT brief leans on energy-sector logistics,
US-Mexico cross-border freight, and Permian Basin operations — the same
Lane-1 mechanic (unrecoverable past) but a fundamentally different commercial
buyer pool.

The brief was authored before camera image URL pattern verification was
possible (the drivetexas.org map XHR layer requires browser-context
inspection, same as 511nj.org). The proposed capture architecture is proven
by analogy to somd-cameras + njdot-511-cameras; the one pre-build step not
yet done is the browser-devtools inspection to identify the camera image
endpoint pattern.

## §5 Lane-1 verification

Per the cerebrum CONSTRAINTS §5 verification methodology, Lane-1 verification
requires checking that the source is not archived by the publisher or any
third party at the cadence the brief depends on:

1. **Upstream archive paths.** Checked drivetexas.org and txdot.gov for any
   `/historical/`, `/archive/`, or similar paths on 2026-05-05. None located.
   TxDOT's open-data portal (data.txdot.gov via Texas Open Data Portal)
   indexes road network and incident data but does NOT include historical
   camera image archives.
2. **Annual rollup / intermediate paths.** No equivalent of NDBC's
   `/data/swden/` monthly+annual rollup pattern exists on drivetexas.org.
   TxDOT does not maintain a parallel image-history pipeline.
3. **Third-party archives.** No commercial vendor offers historical TxDOT
   camera images (verified by reviewing the traffic-data analytics market on
   2026-05-05; INRIX, HERE, Geotab, Wejo all sell aggregated derived metrics
   from camera + sensor input but not raw historical image frames).
4. **Wayback Machine coverage.** Wayback does not archive binary image files
   served at rotating per-camera URLs at any meaningful cadence; this is a
   well-established limitation of the Internet Archive's web-crawler
   infrastructure for image CDNs.
5. **Brief cadence requirement.** The brief targets minute-resolution
   capture (~5-minute polling per camera). No public source preserves images
   at this cadence; a competitor in 2027 cannot reconstruct 2026 frames at
   any price.

§5 hard-floor verdict: **passes.** Defensibility=10 stands; the past is
structurally unrecoverable for any party that didn't capture it in real-time.
