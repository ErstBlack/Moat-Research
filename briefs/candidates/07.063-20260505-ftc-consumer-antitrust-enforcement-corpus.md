## Discovery story

`ftc_consumer_antitrust_enforcement_corpus` was identified during the 2026-05-05
T2 second discovery synthesis pass as part of the explicit effort to add Lane-2
entries beyond the existing USDA APHIS / OSHA / EPA ECHO / MSHA / NLRB cluster.
FTC was chosen because the 2025 political disruption at the FTC is among the
most dramatic and specifically documented of any US regulatory agency this
iteration: the March 2025 commissioner firings (Slaughter v. Trump) are the
subject of active federal litigation, making the Lane-2 political-vulnerability
premise more precisely calibrated than the more diffuse "enforcement reductions"
narrative underlying OSHA or MSHA.

## CONSTRAINTS §5 verification (Lane-2 conditional moat)

1. **Political-vulnerability premise — strongest in corpus.** Three documented
   events create a well-anchored Lane-2 premise:
   - Slaughter v. Trump: active federal litigation contesting the legality of
     commissioner removals in March 2025. Not speculative — a Federal court
     case documents the institutional disruption.
   - FTC Chair Ferguson explicit enforcement-priority statements: on-the-record
     public declarations about de-prioritizing Biden-era tech privacy and
     antitrust cases. Not speculative — specific case reopenings documented in
     FTC press releases.
   - Q1 2025 consent order withdrawals: FTC formally withdrew / transferred
     several Biden-era enforcement proceedings. Specific cases can be identified
     from FTC's own public case status updates.

2. **Detail-layer carve-out — administratively withdrawn cases.** The Lane-2
   moat is specifically the procedural record of ACTIVE cases at the moment of
   withdrawal: complaint narratives, respondents' answers, proposed consent orders,
   analysis-to-aid-public-comment, and supporting harm-theory documents. When a
   case is administratively withdrawn, ftc.gov may reduce the case page to a status
   note ("Matter Closed") without preserving the full procedural record. An archived
   pre-withdrawal snapshot is then the only source of the original documents.

3. **No free-public-archive of full FTC procedural records.** Bloomberg Law,
   Westlaw, and Lexis carry FTC case documents, but these are paid commercial
   tools — they do not trigger §5 per project precedent (Interline, S&P Velocity
   Suite). No free aggregator publishes FTC case documents at full procedural depth.

§5 hard floor: **passes**. Defensibility = 7 (not 0). Composite 7.063 stands.
