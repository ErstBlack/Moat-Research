## Discovery story

`bis_export_enforcement_corpus` was identified during the 2026-05-05 T2 discovery
synthesis pass to diversify the wishlist away from the L2 cluster saturation
(5 Lane-2 entries from prior passes). The BIS/OEE entry adds a Lane-2+4+5
multi-lane candidate in the export-control enforcement vertical — distinct from
the labor/workplace/environmental regulatory verticals that dominate the existing
L2 cluster.

The standout characteristic of this entry is its exceptionally clean legal posture:
`www.bis.gov robots.txt` returned `User-agent: * Allow: /` — explicit unconditional
permission for all crawlers with no path restrictions whatsoever. No other source
in the corpus has this posture; all others have at least Drupal admin path Disallows.
Combined with the trivially small corpus size (~500 MB total historical archive,
~5 MB/month incremental), this entry has the highest implementation and hardware
scores of any Lane-2 brief.

## Rubric calibration note

Composite **7.315** is notable for a Lane-2 brief — significantly above the
existing L2 cluster (6.470–6.805). The high composite reflects the exceptional
hardware fit (9.000 — trivial storage, minimal compute, 2-service stack) and
implementation posture (7.750 — cleanest robots.txt in corpus + fully automatable
small corpus), not a particularly strong financial position. The financial axis
(6.000) is dragged down by the thin ongoing_revenue score (4 — only ~200 new
enforcement orders/year is too thin for subscription-grade recurring value).

This is a defensible pattern: BIS scores above L2 peers on HW/impl because the
corpus is tiny and legally permissive, while scoring comparably to them on the
financial axis because the export-control market is narrower than OSHA/NLRB/FTC
markets. The Lane-2 restriction precedent is moderate (not as strong as APHIS's
2017 database withdrawal); Defensibility=6 is conservative and honest.

## CONSTRAINTS §5 verification (Lane-2 conditional moat)

1. **Political-vulnerability premise — moderate.** OEE enforcement volume
   declined in Q1 2025 (documented in trade press — BIS budget cuts, DOGE
   efficiency initiative, senior staff attrition). The Trump 2017–21 era
   BIS deprioritized enforcement against US-allied country exports. These
   are documented behavioral shifts, but there is NO documented instance of
   BIS removing previously published enforcement orders from the public record
   (contrast: USDA APHIS 2017 database removal). The risk is cumulative omission
   (future actions published in summary form or not at all) rather than retroactive
   withdrawal. Score 6 reflects this moderate precedent.

2. **Raw corpus is publicly reconstructible.** Every enforcement order PDF and
   HTML page is on bis.gov. Any analyst can reconstruct the raw archive. The
   moat is (i) off-platform durability if publication policy shifts and
   (ii) the structured entity graph which requires significant NER/graph
   construction work. §5 is NOT triggered on the raw corpus because the Lane-2
   brief correctly identifies the moat as the off-platform archive + derived
   structure, not duplication of an already-archived corpus.

3. **Lane-4 entity graph secondary.** The structured cross-entity network
   (respondent → intermediary → end-user → country → ECCN) represents one
   Lane-4 pillar (compute-as-barrier for entity extraction + graph assembly)
   without the full three-pillar test being met (no ongoing-update compounding
   at meaningful cadence = ~200/year; no cross-jurisdiction ER in the Lane-4
   sense — BIS is a single US-federal agency). Brief correctly declares Lane 4
   as secondary, not primary. If the Lane-2 premise evaporates entirely, the
   entity-graph residual falls to ~5.5 composite and should be re-evaluated.

§5 hard floor: **passes**. Defensibility = 6 (not 0). Composite 7.315 stands.
