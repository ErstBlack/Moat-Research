## Discovery story

`wsdot_traffic_cameras` was identified during the 2026-05-05 T1 discovery
synthesis pass (iteration `20260505T150802Z-02beae`) as a Lane-1 candidate
explicitly diversifying the lane cluster away from L2 saturation and
extending the Pacific-coast camera coverage that began with the
`caltrans_quickmap_cameras` candidate authored in the same pass.

The pattern observation is identical to how the prior camera briefs were
spotted:

1. WSDOT publishes live camera images from ~700 cameras across WA state
   highways via the WSDOT Traveler Information portal and a free-token-gated
   API.
2. WSDOT does not archive historical images — the traveler-info system is
   explicitly for real-time conditions only, and per WSDOT's public
   documentation images are not preserved.
3. Pacific Northwest commercial corridors are distinct from CA / TX /
   Northeast: NW Seaport Alliance ports (Seattle + Tacoma), I-90 Snoqualmie
   as the only year-round east-west mountain freight route in WA, the
   Microsoft / Amazon tech corridor on SR-520/I-405, and Yakima Valley
   wine + tree-fruit agriculture.

The brief is intentionally smaller-scope than Caltrans (CA): WSDOT's buyer
pool is real but narrower (NW Seaport Alliance < LA/LB combined; Microsoft+
Amazon supply chain < Bay Area aggregate; Yakima ag < Central Valley).
This is honestly reflected in financial axis 6.500 (vs. CA 7.000) without
artificially inflating the moat thesis.

The free-token gating on the WSDOT Traveler Information API is the
distinctive implementation feature of this brief — per cerebrum, free-token
gating is normal authorized use, NOT auth-bypass; the brief-stage
mitigation is reviewing the developer agreement for any §2-territory
clauses (redistribution, archival), which we expect to be compatible with
the private-corpus moat thesis based on the cerebrum precedent across
similar transit-API agreements.

## §5 Lane-1 verification

Per the cerebrum CONSTRAINTS §5 verification methodology, Lane-1
verification requires checking that the source is not archived by the
publisher or any third party at the cadence the brief depends on:

1. **Upstream archive paths.** Checked WSDOT for any `/historical/`,
   `/archive/`, or similar paths on 2026-05-05. None located. WSDOT
   explicitly states on the traveler-info pages that camera images are for
   real-time conditions only and are not preserved. data.wa.gov /
   Washington Open Data Portal indexes some WSDOT datasets (sensor data,
   project tracking) but does NOT include historical camera image archives.
2. **Annual rollup / intermediate paths.** No equivalent of NDBC's
   `/data/swden/` monthly+annual rollup pattern exists on WSDOT
   infrastructure. The traveler-info system is the live-image-serving layer
   only.
3. **Third-party archives.** No commercial or non-commercial vendor offers
   historical WSDOT camera images (verified 2026-05-05; INRIX, HERE,
   Geotab, Wejo, PortVision, Project44 cover aggregated derived metrics
   but not raw historical image frames; PortVision/Project44 cover vessel
   AIS not landside camera).
4. **Wayback Machine coverage.** Wayback does not archive binary image
   files at rotating per-camera CDN URLs at meaningful cadence — well-
   established Internet Archive limitation.
5. **Brief cadence requirement.** The brief targets minute-resolution
   capture (~5-minute polling per camera). No public source preserves
   images at this cadence; a competitor in 2027 cannot reconstruct 2026
   frames at any price.

§5 hard-floor verdict: **passes.** Defensibility=10 stands; the past is
structurally unrecoverable for any party that didn't capture it in
real-time.
