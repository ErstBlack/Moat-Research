## Discovery story

`us_transit_gtfsrt_smaller_agencies` was identified during the 2026-05-04
discovery synthesis pass as the somd-cameras / njdot-511-cameras pattern applied
to transit telemetry. The signal: the same structural conditions that make DOT
camera archives a strong Lane-1 thesis (publicly-published, ephemeral, no
agency archive, unrecoverable past, identifiable buyer pool) are present in
the long tail of US transit-agency VehiclePosition feeds.

The discovery pass identified two key constraints that distinguish this brief
from a naive multi-agency thesis:

1. **Per-agency §5 verification is mandatory at onboarding.** A handful of
   large agencies (NYC MTA, CTA Chicago, BART, WMATA) publish their own
   historical VehiclePosition archives — these fail §5 and are excluded from
   scope by design. The thesis depends on the long tail of agencies that do
   not.

2. **Interline / Transitland is a paid commercial archive, not a public one.**
   Per the §5 strict reading, paid commercial archives do not trigger the
   no-moat hard floor; §5 targets *currently-public archived sources*.
   However, this is the rubric's most aggressive Defensibility=9 (vs. somd-
   cameras' 10) call, and the interpretation should be flagged for operator
   review before promotion to graduated. If §5 is ever tightened to include
   paid commercial archives in the no-moat trigger, this brief auto-rejects.

The "free-token gating ≠ auth-bypass" cerebrum learning was directly produced
by this brief's discovery (AC Transit's 401 response on 2026-05-04), and it
unblocks the entire long-tail agency thesis since most agencies use free-token
gating.

## §5 Lane-1 verification protocol (per-agency, mandatory at onboarding)

Per the cerebrum methodology, Lane-1 §5 verification requires:

1. Read the agency's developer-portal docs for "historical data" / "archive"
   links.
2. Look for both annual rollup and intermediate (monthly/daily) public paths.
3. If the agency publishes any public archive of VehiclePosition payloads at
   <60s cadence, the agency is excluded from scope.
4. Cross-check Mobility Database catalog and Transitland feed registry for
   any publicly-mirrored archive (vs. paid commercial archive — only public
   counts).
5. Document the verification outcome per-agency in the configuration record.

Agencies known a priori to fail §5 and excluded from scope:

- **NYC MTA** (publishes own VehiclePosition + alerts archive at api.mta.info
  + S3 bucket with historical depth).
- **CTA Chicago** (publishes own VehiclePosition archive via CTA developer
  portal).
- **BART** (publishes own real-time API archive).
- **WMATA** (publishes own train + bus VehiclePosition archive).

Wave-1 candidate agencies for explicit per-agency verification at build phase
(in-scope until verified otherwise): AC Transit, RTD Denver, Sound Transit,
Allegheny County PRT (Pittsburgh), Sacramento RT, Cap Metro (Austin), MARTA
(Atlanta), RIPTA (Rhode Island), CDTA (Albany), LANTA (Lehigh Valley),
Foothill Transit, Honolulu DTS, Metra (Chicago commuter rail — verify against
parent CTA scope), King County Metro (Seattle).
