## Discovery story

`cslb_ca_contractor_disciplinary_corpus` was identified during the 2026-05-04
discovery synthesis pass. The signal: California's contractor disciplinary
publication is a high-volume, high-impact public-records source where (a) raw
PDFs are durably archived on CSLB's site (failing any Lane-1 thesis cleanly under
the new §5 constraint), but (b) no public structured corpus exists with the
detail this brief proposes (license number → normalized violation taxonomy →
fine amount → license-status timeline → RMO/RME relationship graph). The pattern
match is the new "Lane 4 entity-resolution as defensibility" cerebrum learning:
when raw is publicly archived but the structured artifact requires substantial
compute + ongoing-update + cross-jurisdiction work, the moat lives in the
processing layer, not the raw access.

The federation roadmap (to NY, FL, TX, and eventually all 50 states) was
identified during the same discovery pass as the natural Lane-4 differentiator
that distinguishes this brief from a one-shot reproducible CA-only analyst job.
The cross-state entity-resolved contractor-identity layer is the moat that
neither LexisNexis Risk nor Verisk currently offer at this depth.

## §5 Lane-4 verification

Per the cerebrum methodology, Lane-4 §5 verification requires the brief to
claim ≥2 of {compute-as-barrier, ongoing-update compounding, cross-jurisdiction
entity resolution}:

1. **Compute-as-barrier — present.** OCR (tesseract or layoutlm) + NER (custom
   model trained on ~500 manually-labeled actions) + entity resolution
   (canonicalization against CSLB license verify endpoint) is genuinely months
   of work. An analyst with the same raw PDFs and standard NLP tooling needs
   ~3–6 months to reach comparable quality, and the result is a one-shot
   analyst artifact, not a compounding live corpus.
2. **Ongoing-update compounding — present.** CSLB publishes new disciplinary
   actions monthly. A live captured + structured corpus is materially more
   valuable each month than the previous month, in ways a one-shot reproduction
   cannot match (e.g., real-time alerting on newly-disciplined contractors,
   trend detection over rolling 12-month windows).
3. **Cross-jurisdiction entity resolution — roadmap, not delivered.** The
   federation to NY, FL, TX, and eventually all 50 states is the strongest
   defensibility upgrade. Until it ships, the moat is weaker. Defensibility=6
   reflects this honestly; re-score to 7–8 once federation to ≥3 states is live.

§5 hard-floor verdict: **passes** (claims all 3, fully on 1+2 and roadmap on 3).
Defensibility = 6, not 0. Composite 7.009 stands.
