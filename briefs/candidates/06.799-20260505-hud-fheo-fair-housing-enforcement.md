## Discovery story

`hud_fheo_fair_housing_enforcement` was identified during the 2026-05-05 T2
discovery synthesis pass alongside BIS and FTC as part of the effort to add
Lane-2 entries beyond the existing L2 cluster. FHEO was chosen because of its
uniquely documented restriction history: two prior site restructurings (2001,
2017) have already removed historical case records, per HUD OIG and NFHA
documentation. This makes the Lane-2 political-vulnerability premise more
precisely anchored in documented repetition than any other L2 entry in the
corpus — only USDA APHIS (single 2017 ACIS database withdrawal) approaches
this specificity.

The FHEO entry is also notable for what it LACKS: no data.gov bulk archive of
case-level narratives, no third-party mirror, no commercial analogue. This means
the Lane-2 carve-out does NOT need to identify a specific detail layer that
bulk archives omit (as OSHA/MSHA/EPA ECHO do) — the ENTIRE case-outcome record
at full procedural depth is the moat, because it simply doesn't exist anywhere
except hud.gov/fheo individual HTML pages and linked PDFs.

## CONSTRAINTS §5 verification (Lane-2 conditional moat)

1. **Political-vulnerability premise — strongest documented restriction
   repetition in the corpus.** Two prior FHEO site restructurings (2001,
   2017) specifically removed historical case records per HUD OIG and NFHA
   documentation. These are not speculative risks — they are verified past
   events from the SAME agency with the SAME type of outcome (case records
   removed during site restructuring). Combined with the 2025 FHEO operational
   changes (budget cuts, enforcement reorientation, staff reductions), the
   structural conditions for a third restructuring event are more concretely
   evidenced here than in any other L2 entry except USDA APHIS.

2. **No bulk-archive carve-out required.** Unlike OSHA/MSHA/EPA ECHO where
   the Lane-2 moat must carve around data.gov bulk archives (which carry
   summary tables but not narrative detail), FHEO has NO parallel data.gov
   archive of case-level narrative detail. This means the ENTIRE full-
   procedural-record layer is the moat — no complex "what fields are in
   the bulk dump vs. what fields are only in the UI" analysis needed.
   Simpler §5 geometry than OSHA/MSHA.

3. **No third-party mirror exists.** Unlike NLRB (Cornell LII mirrors Board
   decisions), FHEO case outcomes have no known free public mirror at
   full case-narrative depth. NFHA publishes advocacy reports using FHEO
   data but not a systematic case archive. This increases the conditional
   moat value (if HUD restricts access, no backup exists) and the operational
   risk (no recovery path if a restriction event occurs during an outage).

§5 hard floor: **passes**. Defensibility = 7 (not 0). Composite 6.799 stands.
