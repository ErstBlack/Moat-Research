## Discovery story

`gdot_ga511_cameras` was identified during the 2026-05-06 T1 discovery synthesis
pass (iteration `20260506T134246Z-edfe37`) as the highest-priority geographic gap
remaining after the FDOT FL511 brief (07.283) closed the Florida SE-corridor gap.
The existing DOT-camera corpus now covers: Pacific Coast (Caltrans CA, WSDOT WA,
ODOT OR), Southwest/Texas interior (TxDOT), mid-Atlantic (somd MD/VA, NJDOT NJ),
and Southeast Florida (FL511). The entire Southeast inland network — Atlanta,
Port of Savannah, and the I-75/I-85 automotive-supply-chain corridors — remained
uncovered.

The Port of Savannah angle distinguishes GDOT from every existing camera brief
except FL511's Port of Miami/Everglades/Tampa Bay coverage. Savannah processes
~6 million TEUs annually (second-busiest East Coast container port) and is the
fastest-growing major US container port by throughput. The I-16/I-95 interchange
and Garden City Terminal access roads (Dean Forest Rd, US-80 corridor) are the
primary overland exit for Southeast US container throughput — a corridor with
direct buyer relevance to the same shipping lines and drayage companies that
value NJDOT's Port of Newark approach roads, but for the Southeast's dominant
container gateway.

The automotive supply-chain angle (Kia Westhaven, Mercedes Vance, BMW Spartanburg,
Hyundai Montgomery) on the I-75 corridor is distinct from all existing camera
briefs. No other brief captures the Southeast's automotive OEM logistics corridors.

The NaviTech/SilverLogic platform used by 511GA is a multi-state vendor deployed
across several US 511 portals. The camera API requires a key for the roster endpoint,
but the standard DOT-camera-corpus methodology (browser-devtools URL-template
discovery of public CDN endpoints) provides an auth-free path to image capture,
confirmed as viable on multiple sister portals. Brief-stage URL-pattern verification
on the NaviTech platform is the primary build-phase pre-condition.

## Rubric calibration note

Composite **7.141** places `gdot_ga511_cameras` just above Caltrans (7.139) and
FL511 (7.283). This ordering reflects:

- **Just above Caltrans (7.139):** Caltrans has more cameras (2,200 vs. 500) and
  a larger California buyer pool (Pacific gateway LA/LB, Bay Area tech, Central
  Valley agriculture — buyer_existence=8 vs. GDOT's 7). However, Caltrans has a
  lower implementation score (CWWP2 individual-page UA filter concern, larger
  solo_operator_load for 2,200 cameras) that pulls it below GDOT's more manageable
  500-camera corpus. GDOT's Port of Savannah buyer angle is distinct enough that a
  minimal composite difference is appropriate; the two briefs serve largely
  non-overlapping geographic markets.

- **Below FL511 (7.283):** FL511 has a multi-seaport marine-freight buyer angle
  (three SE ports: Miami, Everglades, Tampa Bay) that is broader than GDOT's
  single Port of Savannah focus. FL511 also runs on the better-documented
  iCone/511-portal platform (higher source_stability = 7 vs. GDOT's 6), and its
  lower capture cadence (5 captures/hr vs. GDOT's 12/hr at 5-min cadence) produces
  a lower concurrency_cost (9 vs. 8).

- **On par with WSDOT (6.997) + some premium:** WSDOT and GDOT share the same
  implementation profile (6.75) and hardware profile (8.0). GDOT edges above WSDOT
  primarily on financial (6.833 vs. 6.500) because the SE corridor markets
  (Port of Savannah, Atlanta automotive) are larger than the Puget Sound/WA
  market represented by WSDOT.

No rubric changes recommended.

## §5 Lane-1 verification

Per the cerebrum CONSTRAINTS §5 verification methodology:

1. **Upstream archive paths.** Checked 511ga.org and GDOT parent domains for
   any `/historical/`, `/archive/`, or camera-image-history paths on 2026-05-06.
   None located. 511GA is a real-time traveler-information portal; GDOT publishes
   no historical camera image archive.

2. **Annual rollup / intermediate archive paths.** No parallel image-history
   pipeline exists on Georgia DOT infrastructure. GDOT publishes traffic count
   data and crash reports historically, but not camera image archives.

3. **Third-party archives.** No commercial or non-commercial vendor offers
   historical 511GA camera images. INRIX, HERE, StreetLight Data sell derived
   speed/travel-time metrics, not raw historical image frames. No non-commercial
   archive identified.

4. **Wayback Machine coverage.** Same limitation as all DOT camera sister
   briefs — the Internet Archive does not capture binary image files served
   at rotating per-camera CDN URLs at minute resolution.

5. **Brief cadence requirement.** 5-min capture cadence = 12 frames/hr/camera.
   No public source preserves 511GA camera images at this or any other cadence.
   A competitor in 2027 cannot reconstruct 2026 Georgia footage.

§5 hard-floor verdict: **passes.** Defensibility=10 stands.
